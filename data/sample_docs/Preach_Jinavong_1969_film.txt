Preach Jinavong

Year: 1969
Director: Saravuth
Genre: Legendary (boran / folklore legend film)

Cast:
·   Yi Ki Len

Plot Summary (based on the legend the film adapts):
The film adapts the classic Khmer jataka tale of Preah Chinavong (Preah Leak Sinavong). Prince Chinavong is the son of King Sovannavong and Queen Chin Bopha. The king's three concubines, resentful that their own sons receive less respect, plot against the prince: they kill their own children and accuse Chinavong of the murders. The king has Chinavong thrown into the sea, but he is rescued by the Naga king of the underwater realm. There too his virtue attracts jealousy, and the Naga king sends him to seek out the hermit Ta Eisey, from whom he obtains magic powers to use in battle. Like the Reamker, the tale pits a noble kingly hero against the yakkha (ogre) kings of the forest, and it is famous in Khmer court dance for the courteous love dance between Preah Chinavong and Neang Marady.

Overview:
Preach Jinavong is a 1969 Cambodian legendary (boran) film directed by Saravuth, from the golden age of Khmer cinema, adapting the classic Khmer jataka of Preah Chinavong. It has not yet been remade.
