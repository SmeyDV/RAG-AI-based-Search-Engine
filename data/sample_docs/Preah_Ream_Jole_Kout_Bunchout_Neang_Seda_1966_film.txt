Plot Summary (based on the legend the film adapts):
The film draws on the Reamker, the Khmer version of the Ramayana ("Honour of Rama"), whose central figures are Preah Ream (Rama) and his wife Neang Seda (Sita). Prince Preah Ream, though blameless, is banished to the forest for fourteen years together with Neang Seda and his loyal brother Preah Leak. There the demon-king Krong Reap (Ravana) abducts Neang Seda and carries her off to his kingdom of Langka. Preah Ream assembles an army of monkeys led by the general Hanuman, who build a great causeway across the sea to Langka. After Hanuman visits the captive Seda and a mighty battle is fought, Krong Reap is defeated and Preah Ream is reunited with his wife and returns in triumph to claim his throne. The Khmer telling adds episodes beloved in Cambodia, such as Hanuman's encounter with the mermaid Sovanna Maccha.

Overview:
Preah Ream Jole Kout Bunchout Neang Seda is a 1966 Cambodian legendary (boran) film from the golden age of Khmer cinema, adapting an episode of the Reamker (the Khmer Ramayana). It was remade in 2005 as Reamke. (The plot above summarizes the traditional epic the film is based on; no detailed synopsis of this specific 1966 production has been separately published.)
