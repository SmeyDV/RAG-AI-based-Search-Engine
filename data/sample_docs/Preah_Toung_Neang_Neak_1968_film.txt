Preah Toung Neang Neak

Year: 1968
Genre: Legendary (boran / folklore legend film)

Cast:
·   Kong Som Eun
·   Kim Nova

Plot Summary (based on the legend the film adapts):
The film retells the founding legend of Cambodia, the love story of Preah Thong and Neang Neak. Preah Thong is a foreign prince, said to be the son of a Brahman king of the Chola dynasty, who quarrels with his father and sets sail across the ocean, eventually reaching a mysterious island in the land that would become Cambodia. There he sees seven Naga (serpent) princesses bathing along the shore, and falls in love with the youngest and most beautiful, Neang Neak, daughter of the Naga King. He asks for her hand, and her father blesses the marriage by offering part of his kingdom. In the most famous part of the tale, the Naga King drinks up the waters that cover the earth, revealing dry land for the couple and their people to settle - an act traditionally said to have created the Kingdom of Cambodia and given rise to the Khmer people. Their union is still re-enacted in Khmer wedding tradition, where the groom holds the bride's sash as he follows her.

Overview:
Preah Toung Neang Neak is a 1968 Cambodian legendary (boran) film from the golden age of Khmer cinema, adapting Cambodia's founding myth of Preah Thong and the naga princess Neang Neak. It proved enduringly popular and was remade in 1996 and again in 2004.
