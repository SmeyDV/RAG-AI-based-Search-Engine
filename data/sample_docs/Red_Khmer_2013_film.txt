Red Khmer

Year: 2013
Director: Brendan Moriarty
Genre: War / drama

Plot Summary:
Based on true events, the film revolves around Sean Flynn, son of Hollywood legend Errol Flynn, who left Hollywood to make his name as a photojournalist on assignment for TIME magazine and disappeared in Cambodia during the Vietnam War. Foreseeing his own death in captivity, Flynn secretly passes his journals - which contain the harrowing truth about the Khmer Rouge guerrillas - to his trusted friend Lim Po. With the evidence in his hands, Po is forced to risk his life, setting out in search of his family and safety. Trying to evade the Red Khmer, he is confronted by the Royal Cambodian Army, rescues a young girl, and faces deadly obstacles in his fight to survive and reveal the atrocities of war.

Overview:
Red Khmer is a 2013 war drama directed by Brendan Moriarty, a companion piece that concludes the story he began with The Road to Freedom. Filming began on 1 April 2013 in Phnom Penh.
