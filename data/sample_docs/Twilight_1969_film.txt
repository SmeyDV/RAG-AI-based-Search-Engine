Twilight (Khmer: សន្ធិប្រកាស, Sânthĭprâkas; French: Crépuscule)

Year: 1969
Director: Norodom Sihanouk
Genre: Romance / drama

Cast:
·   Norodom Sihanouk as Prince Adit
·   Monique Sihanouk as Maharani Maya
·   Dy Saveth as Sopheap
·   Kong Sam Oeurn as Rudolf Valentino

Plot Summary:
Twilight tells a romantic triangle set against the ancient temples of Angkor. An Indian heroine, the Maharani Maya, visits Cambodia and falls in love with a Khmer prince, Prince Adit. Their romance unfolds amid the ruins of Angkor, with the story drawing inspiration from the archaeological work of Bernard Groslier on the temples' history and architecture. The film uses the grandeur of the monuments as a backdrop for a tale of love, longing and cultural encounter.

Awards:
Won the Golden Apsara Award at the 1969 Phnom Penh International Film Festival, and was screened at the 4th International Film Festival of India.

Overview:
Twilight (French: Crepuscule) is a 1969 Cambodian romantic drama produced, directed, written by and starring Prince Norodom Sihanouk. Running about 75 minutes and made in both Khmer and French, it was distributed by Khemara Pictures and is one of the celebrated features of Cambodia's golden age of cinema.
