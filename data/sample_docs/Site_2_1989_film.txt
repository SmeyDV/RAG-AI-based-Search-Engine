Site 2

Year: 1989
Director: Rithy Panh
Genre: Documentary

Plot Summary:
Site 2 was the largest refugee camp on the Thai-Cambodian border, the inescapable home to some 180,000 Cambodians during the 1980s. Returning to his homeland in 1988, director Rithy Panh met a woman living in the camp, Yim Om, who had lost her entire family to the Khmer Rouge and now lived with those memories in a perpetual exile of loneliness, boredom and fear. The documentary follows the daily life of Yim Om and her family in the camp, narrated by the mother, offering an intimate glimpse into existence as a displaced person.

Overview:
Site 2 is a 1989 documentary directed by Rithy Panh, Cambodia's most internationally acclaimed filmmaker, whose own family was murdered under the Khmer Rouge and who himself passed through the border refugee camps before rebuilding his life in France. It was his first documentary about Cambodian refugees and won several international awards.
