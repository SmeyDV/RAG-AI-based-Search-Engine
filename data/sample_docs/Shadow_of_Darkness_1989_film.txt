Shadow of Darkness (Khmer: ស្រមោលអន្ធកាល, Sror Morl Anthakal)

Year: 1989
Director: Yvon Hem
Genre: Historical drama

Cast:
·   Piseth Pilika

Plot Summary:
The film tells the story of a doctor's family destroyed under the Khmer Rouge regime. Living under Democratic Kampuchea, the family is treated with cruelty by the Khmer Rouge after the youngest son, Visuth, is caught secretly cooking rice in the forest. In punishment, Angkar executes the entire family. Only the eldest son, Visal, survives the massacre, and he escapes, making his way toward the Vietnamese border and freedom.

Overview:
Shadow of Darkness (Khmer: Sror Morl Anthakal) is a 1989 Cambodian historical drama written and directed by Yvon Hem, starring Piseth Pilika. It is historically important as the first Cambodian film set during the Democratic Kampuchea (Khmer Rouge) era - earlier films on the period, such as The Killing Fields, had all been foreign productions. Made in the impoverished conditions of post-Khmer Rouge Cambodia, Yvon Hem reportedly built cameras and equipment from junkyard spare parts, and the film remains one of the few Cambodian works to directly confront the Khmer Rouge genocide.
