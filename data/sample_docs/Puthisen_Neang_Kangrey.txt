Puthisen Neang Kangrey (Khmer: ពុទិសែននាងកង្រី)

Year: 1968
Genre: Legendary / Fantasy (boran / folklore legend film)

Cast:
Kong Som Eun
Virak Dara
Nop Nem
Kim Nova
Saki Sbong
Ly Ratanak

Plot Summary (based on the Twelve Sisters legend the film adapts):
A poor couple, unable to feed their twelve daughters, abandon them in the forest, where they are taken in - and then enslaved - by the ogress Santhomea (Santema). The twelve sisters escape and marry King Rothasith of a nearby kingdom. The vengeful ogress, disguising herself, becomes the king's new consort and, feigning illness, convinces him that only a medicine made from the eyes of his twelve pregnant wives can cure her. The king has eleven of the sisters fully blinded and the twelfth, Neang Pou, left with one eye; the blinded women are imprisoned in a cave, where in their hunger and despair they eat their newborn children. Only Neang Pou's son, Puthisen (also called Rithisen), survives. He grows up and travels to the ogress's land; she sends him on with a letter secretly ordering that he be killed, but a kindly hermit alters it to command instead that Puthisen marry her daughter, Neang Kangrey (Kong Rei). Kangrey falls deeply in love with him. Puthisen learns that his mothers' stolen eyes are kept in the treasury; he seizes them and flees. As Kangrey pursues him, he throws down magic medicine that turns into a vast sea, cutting off her path. Neang Kangrey weeps for her lost love until she dies of grief, and her body becomes the Kangrey mountain in Kampong Chhnang, which still resembles a woman lying on her back.

Overview:
Puthisen Neang Kangrey (Khmer: ពុទិសែននាងកង្រី; lit. "Puthisen and Lady Kangrey", also called "Twelve Sisters") is a 1968 Cambodian film, a classic of Cambodian cinema based on the popular Khmer myth of the Twelve Sisters, whose tragic heroine is Lady Kangrey, after whom a mountain range in Kampong Chhnang is named. The lead cast included Kong Som Eun and Virak Dara, and the film has been re-released twice, in 2000 and 2002. (The plot above summarizes the traditional legend the film is based on.)
