Preah Ko Preah Keo

Year: 1965
Genre: Legendary (boran / folklore legend film)

Plot Summary (based on the legend the film adapts):
The film adapts the famous Cambodian legend of Preah Ko and Preah Keo, two miraculous brothers born in Cambodia: the elder, Preah Ko, is a sacred ox whose belly holds precious treasures and divine power, and the younger, Preah Keo, is a man. Together they bring peace and prosperity wherever they live - Preah Ko can bellow to fill enemy soldiers with dread, summon rain during drought, and sense truth from lies - forming a spiritual defense for the Khmer kingdom through prophecy, blessing, and protection from invasion and disaster. The king of Siam, coveting their powers, sends a great army to seize them. In the tale's tragic conclusion the two sacred brothers are captured and carried off, and legend holds that they remain held captive in Siam to this day - their loss mirroring the fate of the Khmer land in the troubled post-Angkorian era.

Overview:
Preah Ko Preah Keo is a 1965 Cambodian legendary (boran) film, one of the films of 1965 that mark the beginning of the golden age of Khmer cinema. It adapts the beloved Cambodian legend of the sacred bull Preah Ko and his brother Preah Keo.
