Shadow over Angkor (French: Ombre sur Angkor)

Year: 1968
Director: Norodom Sihanouk
Genre: Political thriller / drama

Cast:
·   Norodom Sihanouk (also producer, writer and composer)

Plot Summary:
The film uncovers an international plot to topple the Cambodian government. The conspiracy is fomented from outside the country and from within the Angkor-Siem Reap-Kompong Thom region, backed by the CIA, two foreign governments tied to imperialism, and a treacherous regional governor and army commander. The Cambodian army and the state secret service work together to expose and foil the plot, and the nation vows to destroy any aggressor that threatens its independence.

Awards:
Screened at the 1969 Moscow International Film Festival.

Overview:
Shadow over Angkor (French: Ombre sur Angkor) is a 1968 Cambodian political thriller produced, directed, written by and starring Prince Norodom Sihanouk. Made during the golden age of Cambodian cinema, it was one of nine feature films Sihanouk directed between 1966 and 1969, and reflects the political tensions of the era shortly before the escalation of the war in the region.
