Kraitoung Kropeu Charavan

Year: 1968
Director: Dy Saveth
Genre: Legendary (boran / folklore legend film)

Cast:
·   Kong Som Eun
·   Dy Saveth

Plot Summary (based on the legend the film adapts):
The film adapts the classic crocodile legend of Kraitoung (Krai Thong) and the crocodile lord Chorlavan (Chalawan). In an underwater cave kingdom ruled by the great crocodile lord Chorlavan, crocodiles take human form and want for nothing. When the daughters of a wealthy man ignore warnings and swim in the river, Chorlavan emerges, falls madly in love, and abducts one of them - Tapao Thong - to his cave, casting a spell to make her love him and become his wife. No one can defeat the crocodile lord until Kraitoung, a man trained to hunt crocodiles, volunteers to rescue her. Armed with a magical spear given by his teacher, he descends to the crocodile's lair, stabs Chorlavan in the back, and pursues the badly wounded beast until he finally kills him, returning to the surface with the rescued woman.

Overview:
Kraitoung Kropeu Charavan is a 1968 Cambodian legendary (boran) film directed by golden-age star Dy Saveth, who also stars alongside Kong Som Eun. It adapts the popular crocodile legend of Kraitoung and the crocodile lord Chorlavan, a folktale shared across mainland Southeast Asia. It has not yet been remade.
