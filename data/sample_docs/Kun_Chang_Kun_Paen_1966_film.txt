Kun Chang Kun Paen

Year: 1966
Director: Ly Va
Genre: Legendary (boran / folklore legend film)

Cast:
·   Kong Som Eun
·   Pka Rozeth

Plot Summary (based on the legend the film adapts):
The film adapts the classic Southeast Asian folk epic Khun Chang Khun Phaen, a love triangle set among three childhood friends. Khun Phaen is handsome, clever and skilled in warfare and sorcery, but poor, his father having been executed and his family's property seized; Khun Chang is ugly and dim-witted, but rich and well-connected at court; and Wanthong is the beautiful woman both men love. Khun Phaen and Wanthong marry, but Khun Chang schemes to have his rival sent to war and then falsely reports him dead, taking Wanthong into his own house. When Khun Phaen returns - now with another wife - jealous quarrels drive Wanthong to Khun Chang and his comforts. Their decades-long rivalry runs through wars, abductions, court cases, trial by ordeal, jail and treachery. In the tragic ending, the king, seeing Wanthong as a woman of "two hearts" torn between the two men, orders her execution.

Overview:
Kun Chang Kun Paen is a 1966 Cambodian legendary (boran) film directed by Ly Va, from the golden age of Khmer cinema, adapting the classic epic tale of Khun Chang Khun Phaen. It was remade in 2007.
