Year: 1972
Genre: Legendary (boran / folklore legend film)

Cast:
·   Kong Som Eun

Plot Summary (based on the legend the film adapts):
The film draws on the Khmer legend of the Twelve Sisters, whose hero is Rithisen (also called Puthisen). Rithisen is the only surviving son of twelve sisters who, after marrying a king, were blinded through the scheming of the ogress Santhomea and imprisoned in a cave. Grown to manhood, Rithisen journeys to the ogress's land and, thanks to a hermit who alters a letter meant to have him killed, instead marries the ogress's daughter, Neang Kangrey (Kong Rei), who falls in love with him. Learning that his mothers' stolen eyes are hidden in the treasury, Rithisen recovers them and flees; when Kangrey chases after him, he casts down magic medicine that becomes a vast sea barring her path, and she weeps herself to death, her body becoming the mountain of Kangrey in Kampong Chhnang.

Overview:
Rithisen Prune Meas is a 1972 Cambodian legendary (boran) film from the golden age of Khmer cinema, drawing on the Twelve Sisters legend and its hero Rithisen (Puthisen). It has not yet been remade.
