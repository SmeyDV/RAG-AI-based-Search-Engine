Poppy Goes to Hollywood

Year: 2016
Director: Sok Visal
Genre: Comedy / comedy-drama

Plot Summary:
Mony is a young layabout who hangs around the streets of Phnom Penh until the day he witnesses a murder, forcing him to go into hiding. He seeks refuge with his older brother, a transgender performer in a drag club. Now on the run with his brother's friends and fellow performers, Mony disguises himself as a dancer, takes the name Poppy, and hides out at Hollywood, a nightclub in the Cambodian countryside. With both the mob and the police searching for him, Mony has to adapt to his new identity and the unfamiliar world he has been thrown into.

Overview:
Poppy Goes to Hollywood is a 2016 Cambodian comedy directed by Sok Visal, released in Cambodia on 5 May 2016. Blending humor with moments of heartache, it is noted as one of the first Cambodian feature films to center LGBT characters and themes. A recut version, Poppy Goes to Hollywood Redux, later screened internationally, including at the Busan International Film Festival.
