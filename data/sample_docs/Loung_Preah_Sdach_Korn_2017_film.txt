Loung Preah Sdach Korn (Khmer: ហ្លួងព្រះស្តេចកន)

Year: 2017
Director: Mao Ayuth
Genre: Historical epic / action / drama

Plot Summary:
The film dramatizes the life of Sdach Korn, a commoner who rose through the ranks to become one of the kings of Cambodia in the early sixteenth century. Set in a period of court intrigue and civil war, the story follows the reign of King Srey Sukonthor and the rebellion that ultimately overthrows the crown, bringing its leader, Sdach Korn, to the throne. The narrative combines large-scale battle sequences, palace politics and personal ambition to depict a turbulent and important chapter in Cambodian history.

Overview:
Loung Preah Sdach Korn (Khmer: ហ្លួងព្រះស្តេចកន) is an ambitious 2017 Cambodian historical epic directed by veteran filmmaker Mao Ayuth. One of the largest local productions of its time, it was the final film of Mao Ayuth's long career and is regarded as a landmark attempt to bring a major episode of Cambodian history to the big screen.
