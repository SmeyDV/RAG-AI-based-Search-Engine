Blank Page (French: Page blanche)

Year: 1991
Director: Hồ Quang Minh
Genre: Drama / war / history

Plot Summary:
Vixna, a Cambodian woman living in exile in Paris, is lured back to Cambodia with her two children by her husband, an officer in Pol Pot's army. On arrival she is immediately taken into custody and separated from her children, and learns that her husband has been killed. She is sent to a Khmer Rouge commune and forced into heavy labour for the regime's "agricultural miracle," enduring brutal conditions - including having to bone corpses to make glue - until the Vietnamese liberation of 1979.

Overview:
Blank Page (French: Page blanche) is a 1991 drama directed by Hồ Quang Minh and shot on authentic locations in Cambodia. It portrays the horrors of the Cambodian killing fields under the Khmer Rouge through the eyes of a woman who returns from exile, and screened at the International Film Festival Rotterdam in 1992.
