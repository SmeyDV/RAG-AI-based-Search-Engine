The Golden Voice

Year: 2006
Director: Gregory Cahill
Genre: Drama, Short Film
Country: United States (filmed in Cambodia and Los Angeles)

Cast:
Sophea Pel as Ros Serey Sothear
Narin Pot as Chenda
Chai Yong as Khmer Rouge Leader
Daren Thach as Male Soldier
Theavy Van as Female Soldier

Plot Summary:
The Golden Voice is a short drama film about the famous Cambodian rock singer Ros Serey Sothea and her struggle for survival during the Khmer Rouge regime. Ros Serey Sothea was one of Cambodia's most beloved singers during the golden age of Khmer music in the 1960s and 1970s, known for her powerful and distinctive voice. The film depicts how she learns that her singing voice is her only chance to overcome the brutal Pol Pot regime that systematically targeted artists, intellectuals, and performers.

Production:
The film was shot on location in Cambodia and in Los Angeles, California. It was produced by Rising Falcon Cinema and written, directed, and produced by Gregory Cahill. The film brings attention to the tragic story of Ros Serey Sothea, whose fate under the Khmer Rouge remains one of the enduring mysteries of Cambodia's lost generation of artists.

Awards:
The Golden Voice won multiple awards including Special Recognition at the LA Shorts Fest, Best Short Film at CamboFest, and Best Short Film at the San Joaquin Film Festival. It was also an official selection at numerous film festivals including the Rhode Island International Film Festival, Dallas Asian Film Festival, Woods Hole Film Festival, Boulder Asian Film Festival, Beverly Hills Film Festival, New Orleans Film Festival, San Diego Asian Film Festival, DisOrient Film Festival, and FirstGlance Philadelphia Film Festival.

Reception:
The film has been praised for bringing international attention to the story of Ros Serey Sothea, one of Cambodia's most iconic musical figures whose life was tragically cut short by the Khmer Rouge regime. It serves as both a tribute to her artistic legacy and a reminder of the cultural devastation wrought by the genocide.

Overview:
The Golden Voice is a 2006 American short drama film about Ros Serey Sothea, the legendary Cambodian singer from the golden age of Khmer rock and roll. The film was shot in Cambodia and Los Angeles and won several film festival awards. It explores themes of artistic expression under totalitarian rule and the power of one's voice as a means of survival.
