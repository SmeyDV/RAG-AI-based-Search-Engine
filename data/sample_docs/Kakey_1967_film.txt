Plot Summary (based on the legend the film adapts):
The film adapts the classical Khmer tale of Neang Kakey (Kaki), a story of an unfaithful wife best known from the verse melodrama composed by the future King Ang Duong in 1815. In the kingdom of Peareansey, under King Prohmtoat, the royal consort Neang Kakey - the first and most beautiful of the king's concubines - is secretly carried off each night by Garuda, the king's own counselor, who has tricked his way into her bed. Garuda takes her to his kingdom. When another royal counselor, Kanthан, is sent to rescue her, he instead becomes her daytime lover, while Garuda, unaware, remains only her lover by night. Garuda eventually discovers her betrayal and casts her out; the king repudiates her without mercy. Set adrift alone on a boat, Neang Kakey drifts on the sea for seven days, weeping, before she is swallowed by the waves. In Khmer culture the name "Kakey" has since become a byword for a woman unfaithful to her man.

Overview:
Kakey is a 1967 Cambodian legendary (boran) film directed by Biv Chai Leang, from the golden age of Khmer cinema, adapting the well-known classical Khmer tale of Neang Kakey.
