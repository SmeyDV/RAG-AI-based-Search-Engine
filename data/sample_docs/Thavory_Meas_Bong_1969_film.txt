Thavory Meas Bong (Khmer: ថាវរីមាសបង)

Year: 1969
Director: Uong Citta (also known as Uon Kon Thuok / Uong Kanthouk)
Genre: Melodrama / romance

Cast:
·   Kong Som Eun
·   Saom Vansodany
·   Vichara Dany
·   So Hean

Plot Summary:
When Socheat and Thavary marry, their union disappoints two other people: Sunny, Thavary's sister, who is in love with Socheat, and Sorin, Socheat's friend, who is in love with Thavary. Bitter and jealous, Sunny and Sorin hatch a plot to break the couple apart by convincing Thavary that she has contracted leprosy. Their scheme sets off a chain of tragic misunderstandings that threatens to destroy the marriage and the lives around it.

Overview:
Thavory Meas Bong (Khmer: ថាវរីមាសបង, roughly "Sister of Gold") is a 1969 Cambodian melodrama directed by Uong Citta. Its songs were performed by the legendary singer Sinn Sisamouth. A product of Cambodia's golden age of cinema, it is one of the rare films to have survived the Khmer Rouge era, during which most Cambodian films were lost or destroyed; only three of the director's six successful features are known to survive.
