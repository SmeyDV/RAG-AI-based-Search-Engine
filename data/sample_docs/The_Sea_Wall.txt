The Sea Wall (French: Un barrage contre le Pacifique)

Year: 2008
Director: Rithy Panh
Genre: Drama, Historical Fiction, Literary Adaptation
Country: France, Cambodia, Belgium

Overview:
The Sea Wall is a fiction film adapted from a novel by Marguerite Duras. Set in French colonial Indochina, it follows a widowed mother and her children living on an unsuccessful rice concession threatened by the sea. The family confronts poverty, colonial power, and difficult personal relationships. The film marked one of Rithy Panh's fictional works before his later return to fiction with Meeting with Pol Pot.

Reception:
The film was released in 2008 and presented at the Toronto International Film Festival.
