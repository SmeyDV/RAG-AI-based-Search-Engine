A River Changes Course

Year: 2013
Director: Kalyanee Mam
Genre: Documentary
Country: Cambodia
Language: Khmer, Jarai
Runtime: 83 minutes

Cast (subjects):
Sari Math
Khieu Mok
Sav Samourn

Synopsis:
A River Changes Course intimately captures the stories of three families living in Cambodia as they strive to maintain their traditional ways of life amid rapid development and environmental degradation. Deep in the jungle, Sav Samourn struggles as large companies encroach and "progress" claims the life-giving forests. She discovers there is little room for wild animals, ghosts, and the home she has always known. In a fishing hamlet, Sari Math must quit school to help support his family. But as the fish catch dwindles, Sari and his family find their livelihood threatened. In a village, Khieu Mok must leave to seek work in a Phnom Penh factory to help pay her family's debts. But city life proves no better, and Khieu struggles between her need to send money home and her duty to be with her loved ones. From Cambodia's forests to its rivers, from its idyllic rice fields to the capital's pulsing heart, forces of radical change are transforming the landscape of the country and the dreams of its people. The film's original Khmer title, Kbang Tik Tonle, is the traditional practice of dipping one's hands into the water and drinking the water with both hands. This single act connects the Cambodian people to the water, to nature, and ultimately to life.

Reception:
The New York Times selected A River Changes Course as a "NYT Critics' Pick." Nicole Herrington found the film to be "a beautiful example of the form. Instead of delving into the politics of their plight, Ms. Mam delivers a universal story" that "this human story is profound enough to stand on its own." Joe Morgenstern of The Wall Street Journal wrote: "Beautiful images can be a distraction in a serious documentary, but that's hardly the case here. They draw us in so we can better understand the hurtling changes that endanger the future of Cambodia." Sheri Linden of the Los Angeles Times noted that "Mam's camera work is exquisite in its immediacy and agility." The Huffington Post called the film "breathtakingly beautiful...breaks new ground."

Overview:
A River Changes Course is a 2013 documentary by Cambodian-American filmmaker Kalyanee Mam that explores the damage rapid development has wrought in Cambodia on both a human and environmental level. The film follows three families as they struggle to maintain traditional ways of life amid forced displacement, deforestation, and industrialization. It won the Grand Jury Prize for World Cinema Documentary at the 2013 Sundance Film Festival and numerous other awards.
