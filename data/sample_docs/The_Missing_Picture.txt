The Missing Picture (French: L'Image manquante)

Year: 2013
Director: Rithy Panh
Genre: Documentary, Animation, History
Country: Cambodia, France
Runtime: 95 minutes

Overview:
Rithy Panh reflects on childhood, memory, and life under Khmer Rouge rule between 1975 and 1979. Because images capable of representing the experience are absent, the film combines archival material with painted clay figures and constructed scenes. The result treats cinema as a way to search for and create a missing historical image while remembering victims of the Cambodian genocide.

Awards and Reception:
The film won the Un Certain Regard Prize at the 2013 Cannes Film Festival. It was nominated for Best Foreign Language Film at the 86th Academy Awards, becoming the first Cambodian film nominated in that category.
