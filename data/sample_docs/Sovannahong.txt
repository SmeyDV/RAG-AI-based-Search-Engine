Sovannahong (Khmer: សុវណ្ណហង្ស; Golden Phoenix)

Year: 1967
Director: Yvon Hem
Genre: Fantasy

Cast:

Plot Summary:
Sovannahong draws on Khmer mythology and legend, telling the story of a giant golden phoenix (the Sovannahong) that possesses flight and aircraft-like qualities. Blending romance, magic and adventure in the classic boran (legend film) tradition, it follows a meandering and at times violent fantasy tale rooted in Cambodian folklore. The film was one of the most commercially successful local fantasy productions of the 1960s, celebrated for its imaginative storytelling and its songs. Its soundtrack featured original compositions performed by well-known singers of the era, including Meas Kok and Huoy Meas.

Overview:
Sovannahong (Golden Phoenix) is a 1967 Cambodian fantasy film directed by Yvon Hem. Made during the golden age of Khmer cinema, it stands among the most successful fantasy and legend films of the Sangkum era, drawing directly on traditional Khmer mythology.
